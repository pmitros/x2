Statistical Significance Tests

Statistical significance tests help us solve problems like the following:

The average SAT score in Mathematics is μ=514 with standard deviation of σ=117 . A math teacher in Springfield High has a class of 30 students who have received scores as follows:

The teacher wants to find out whether his students were better than average, which would mean that he did a good job teaching. He calculates the average score of his class, let's call it the "class average": S=543, which is obviously higher than the nation average μ=514.

However, the teacher is still not convinced. He is worried that the higher scores might be a fluke due to randomness, and that his students are actually average. What the teacher wants to calculate is the probability that if the students are average they will get scores at least as good as the ones they got. In statistics this probability is called a p-value. If the p-value is sufficiently small (less than 0.05), we will reject the hypothesis that the students are average, which in our case would mean that the students are better.
