The teacher wants to find out whether his students were better than average, which would mean that he did a good job teaching. He is worried that the higher scores might be a fluke due to randomness, and that his students are actually average. What the teacher wants to calculate is the probability that if the students are truly average they will receive the scores they did. In statistics this probability is called a p-value.
